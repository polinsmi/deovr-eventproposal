Part I · Section 04

Venue & vendors

Step 1 — Request & compare

I'd start with the proposed hotel options (Mama Shelter Belgrade and Hotel Bristol) and request pricing, floor plans, F&B packages, AV inclusions, and availability. As a backup, I'd also look at a separate coworking space (e.g. Impact Hub Belgrade) for work sessions combined with a hotel for accommodation.

Selection criteria

Space flexibility
Can it support plenary + breakout + lounge simultaneously?
AV quality
Screens, sound, WiFi bandwidth sufficient for 20 people working in parallel
Overall design
Not a generic conference room — something that feels alive. Both creative and functional.
F&B setup
Separate dining space; flexibility on menus and timing. Menu must include options for vegetarians, vegans, and common allergies.
Location
Walkable to evening restaurant options
Overall offer
Best combination of price, included services, and flexibility

Step 2 — Negotiate

  1. Room + event space bundle. Book 20 rooms / 5 nights since hotels are more likely to include event spaces at a discount when the room block is this size.
  2. F&B. Review the menu and confirm options for vegetarians, vegans, and common allergies. Push for a discount on the overall package.
  3. AV package. Clarify what's included and pricing: projectors, screens, mics, WiFi.
  4. Cancellation policy. Push for a reasonable cancellation window — at minimum 2–3 weeks notice without penalty.
  5. Option C (coworking for work sessions + hotel for accommodation). Keep as a backup — in case hotel pricing or availability doesn't work out.

Part II · Section 06

DeoVR Creator & Partners Open House

One possible concept — the final format depends on the answers to the questions above. This concept is built as live Q&A and open tables from multiple players, not a DeoVR monologue.

Programme — 3 hours

Check-in & networking
20 min
Guests arrive and check in. Bar and food station open. VR stations open for demos.
Opening & announcements
15–20 min
Short welcome from DeoVR — product updates, news, or announcements. Partners can also share brief updates relevant to the audience.
Three Open Corners
60 min
Space splits into three simultaneous zones — guests move freely:

Corner 1 — DeoVR Platform — publishing, monetisation, analytics, promo.
Corner 2 — Production & Hardware — studios and hardware partners on setups, challenges, test options.
Corner 3 — Brands & Deals — brands on what formats they need and how creators can get involved.
Open bar & networking
Until close
Closing remarks. Followed by open networking with food and bar.

Location & venue

Location
Major tech hub city with a dense VR and spatial media community. Ideally timed around an existing industry event to reduce acquisition cost.
Venue
Not a hotel ballroom — raw, architectural, or purpose-built for immersive experiences. Somewhere that allows VR stations, open flow between zones, and a bar without feeling like a conference.

Marketing & distribution

T−6w Partners confirmed  ·  T−5w Media & influencers  ·  T−4w Announce via social + partner channels  ·  T−3w Direct invites via Luma or LinkedIn (LN Helper)

Part II · Section 08

Working with external partners

1. Prep
Define what this partner brings and what we offer in return. Build a shortlist, keep only the ones that fit.
2. Outreach
Through our network where possible.
3. Align
Roles, contributions, budget split if co-hosting. Put it in writing — no verbal-only agreements.
4. Execute
Shared timeline, clear owners. Adjust as needed along the way.
5. Post-event
Share results, collect feedback.
